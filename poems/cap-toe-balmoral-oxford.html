A « mélange explosif de fausse subversion et d'ultra-conservatisme »

Let, he said, me get,
my shoes. He said, Let me
get my shoes. Image citation, hereafter, cap-toe balmoral oxfords.
A WebP of these, on idle loop—endless.

Notes

  • The title is from, “Fascisme. Fascisation. Antifascisme.” English translation: “an explosive mixture of false subversion and ultra-conservatism,” from “Fascism, Fascisation, Antifascism.”
  • The opening lines, a quote: “Let me get my shoes. Let me get my shoes.” From, “Pop, pop, pop, then a bloodied Trump rushed from election rally.”
  • An example of cap-toe balmoral oxfords.
  • The closing lines, from Orwell's 1984,” “If you want a picture of the future, imagine a boot stamping on a human face—forever.”
  • WebP is an image file format created by Google, a company that donated $1mil to the second inauguration of Donald Trump.
  • An “idle loop” is a lengthy, and in some cases, infinite form of background processing. Also: the halting problem. Stephen Cole Kleene, Introduction to Metamathematics, 1952: "there is no algorithm for deciding whether any given machine, when started from any given initial situation, eventually stops."
